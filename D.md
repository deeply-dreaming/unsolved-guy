D1: Sums of like powers (Euler): Are there parametric solutions and counterexamples in higher powers (e.g., a^5 + b^5 + c^5 + d^5 = e^5, and beyond)?
D2: Fermat problem (regular primes): Are there infinitely many regular primes (equivalently, primes not dividing the relevant Bernoulli numerators / class numbers)?
D3: Figurate numbers: Is every sufficiently large integer a sum of three positive pentagonal numbers (or three positive hexagonal numbers)?
D4: Waring’s problem: Find g(k) and G(k), the minimal number of k-th powers needed to represent all (or all sufficiently large) integers.
D5: Sums of cubes: Is every integer a sum of four cubes, and are all integers not of the form 9n ± 4 sums of three cubes?
D6: Ljunggren’s equation: Find an elementary proof that y^2 = 2x^4 - 1 has only the positive integer solutions (x,y) = (1,1) and (13,239).
D7: Erdos-Moser (Bowen) equation: Does 1^n + 2^n + ··· + (m - 1)^n = m^n have any nontrivial integer solutions?
D8: A pyramidal Diophantine equation: Find (parametrize) all integer solutions of x^3 + y^3 + z^3 = x + y + z.
D9: Hall’s conjecture / gaps between powers: How small can |x^3-y^2| be (e.g., relative to x^{1/2}), and what lower bounds exist for gaps between perfect powers?
D10: Exponential Diophantine equations: When can equations of the form ∑ ± p_i^{a_i} = 0 be decided by congruences, and what are solutions to (q^x - 1)/(q - 1) = p^y?
D11: Egyptian fractions (Erdos-Straus): Is 4/n = 1/x + 1/y + 1/z solvable in positive integers for every n > 1?
D12: Markoff numbers: Does each Markoff number z occur in a unique solution (x, y, z) to x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 3xyz?
D13: The equation x^x y^y = z^z: Besides trivial solutions, what are all integer solutions (did Ko find them all)?
D14: ai + bj made squares: For given m,n, can one choose integers ai and bj so that all sums ai + bj are perfect squares?
D15: Pairwise sums are squares: For every n, do there exist n distinct integers such that the sum of any two is a perfect square?
D16: Triples with the same sum and the same product: For a given multiplicity, what are the smallest common sums/products admitting that many distinct triples?
D17: Disjoint blocks of consecutive integers: Can the product of two (or more) disjoint blocks of consecutive integers be a perfect power?
D18: Perfect cuboid: Does there exist a rectangular box with integer edges, integer face diagonals, and an integer body diagonal?
D19: Rational distances from a square: Is there a point whose distances to all four corners of the unit square are rational?
D20: Six (or more) points at rational distances: Can a 6-point rational-distance configuration in general position be extended to more points?
D21: Integer triangle with integer medians and area: Does a triangle exist with integer sides, integer medians, and integer area?
D22: Simplexes with rational contents: Do there exist higher-dimensional simplexes with all edges/areas/volumes rational, and can tetrahedra be approximated by such?
D23: Some quartic equations: Are there four triangular numbers in geometric progression (equivalently, certain nontrivial quartic Diophantine solutions)?
D24: Sum equals product: For k-tuples of positive integers with product = sum, how many solutions N(k) are there (and does N(k) → ∞)?
D25: Factorials: Are the only solutions to n! + 1 = x^2 given by n = 4, 5, 7?
D26: Fibonacci numbers of various shapes: Which Fibonacci/Lucas numbers are squares, polygonal numbers, or other special forms (and how to prove such classifications)?
D27: Congruent numbers: Is Tunnell’s criterion also sufficient (i.e., does it exactly characterize congruent numbers)?
D28: A reciprocal Diophantine equation: Find all integer solutions to 1/w + 1/x + 1/y + 1/z + 1/(wxyz) = 0.
D29: Diophantine m-tuples: Does a Diophantine 5-tuple with property D(1) exist (and how large can m be in general)?
