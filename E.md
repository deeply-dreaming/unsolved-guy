E1: Thin sequence with prime completion: Does there exist a sequence A with A(x) < c ln x such that every sufficiently large integer is p + a (p prime, a in A)?
E2: LCM-bounded sequences: What is the maximum possible A(x) for a sequence {ai} such that lcm(ai, aj) ≤ x for every pair?
E3: Comparable divisors: Is the natural density of integers having divisors d1 < d2 < 2d1 equal to 1?
E4: No term divides a product of r others: For r≥2, how large can A(x) be if no ai divides the product of r other terms?
E5: Covering by divisibility: For a finite set a1 < ··· < ak ≤ n with D(x)=#{m ≤ x divisible by some ai}, is D(x)/x < 2·D(n)/n for all x > n?
E6: Avoiding a dense target sumset: If {ni} is “well distributed” (ni+1/ni→1 and uniform mod d) and {aj} has no aj+ak equal to any ni, must the density of {aj} be < 1/2?
E7: Alternating prime series: Does ∑ (-1)^n n/p_n converge, and in ordered products of powers of fixed primes can consecutive terms both be prime powers infinitely often?
E8: No pair-sum a square: How large can a subset {a1<…<ak}⊆[1,n] be if no sum ai+aj is a perfect square (conjecturally k ≤ n/3 + O(1))?
E9: Roth-type partition sumsets: Partition [1,n] into k classes; must within-class sums produce ≥ c·n distinct integers ≤ n? What about products (open for k=2)?
E10: Van der Waerden / Szemeredi: Bound W(h,l) and r_k(n) (k-term arithmetic progression thresholds), e.g. conjecture r_k(n)=o(n/(ln n)^t) for every t>0.
E11: Schur numbers: Find s(n), the largest m such that [1,m] can be partitioned into n sum-free classes (no x+y=z within a class).
E12: Modular Schur numbers: Find t(n), the largest m such that [1,m] can be partitioned into n classes with no x+y≡z (mod m+1) within a class (is t(n)=s(n))?
E13: Strongly sum-free partitions: Find s(m,n) / r(n) for partitions of intervals into n classes avoiding x+y=z and/or x+y+1=z within a class.
E14: Rado regularity: For ∑ ai xi = 0, how does the minimal u(n) forcing a monochromatic solution grow, and do k-regular but not (k+1)-regular equations exist for every k?
E15: Gobel recursion integrality: For which starting values/powers does Gobel’s recursion produce only integers, and where does the first noninteger occur?
E16: Collatz (3x+1): Does the iteration n→n/2 (even), n→3n+1 (odd) reach 1 for every starting integer?
E17: Permutation iteration sequences: For the map 2m→3m, 4m-1→3m-1, 4m+1→3m+1, do infinite chains exist and what cycles/chains occur (e.g., the sequence containing 8)?
E18: Mahler’s Z-numbers: Does there exist a real a such that the fractional parts of a(3/2)^n all lie in [0,1/2) for every n?
E19: Primes in ⌊(4/3)^n⌋ and ⌊(3/2)^n⌋: Do these sequences contain infinitely many primes?
E20: Davenport-Schinzel sequences: For given n and order d, find N_d(n), the maximum length of a sequence on n symbols avoiding long alternations.
E21: Thue-Morse / avoidable repetitions: How far can one avoid squares/overlaps and permutation-adjacent blocks in infinite words?
E22: Permutation-supersequence cycles: Find minimal m(n) for a cycle on {1,…,n} containing every permutation of {1,…,n} as a subsequence (conj. m(n)≤⌊n^2/2⌋).
E23: Covering integers by arithmetic progressions: If a union of n arithmetic progressions with differences ≥ k covers [1, k^2 n - k + 1], must it cover all positive integers?
E24: Irrationality sequences: Characterize sequences {an} such that ∑ 1/(an bn) is irrational for every integer sequence {bn}, and find interesting examples.
E25: Self-describing / counting sequences: Prove asymptotics and structure for Golomb’s self-describing sequence and related counting/self-descriptive constructions.
E26: Put-or-Take-a-Square: In Epstein’s game where you add/subtract the largest square ≤ the heap, do the sets of P-positions or N-positions have positive density?
E27: Max and mex sequences: For sequences extended by max rules or mex rules, are such sequences ultimately periodic (especially mex sequences)?
E28: B2 / Sidon sequences: For sequences with all pairwise sums distinct, what is sup ∑ 1/ai (and what sequences attain it)?
E29: Sums and products in 2-colorings: In a 2-coloring of the integers, can an increasing sequence have all pairwise sums ai+aj and products aiaj in one color?
E30: MacMahon’s primes of measurement: For the sequence formed by excluding sums of consecutive earlier terms, estimate growth of m_n and whether its density is 0.
E31: Hofstadter sequences: For Hofstadter’s recurrences (and “dual” variants), how do they grow and which integers do they miss?
E32: Greedy sum-free / B2 sequences (Dickson/Stohr): For sequences built by greedy exclusion of pairwise sums, are the differences an+1-an ultimately periodic?
E33: Monotone arithmetic progression avoidance: Is M(n)^{1/n} bounded, and must every permutation of the positive integers contain a monotone 4-term arithmetic progression?
E34: Happy numbers: Prove bounds on the density of base-10 happy numbers and on runs/gaps (e.g., arbitrarily long consecutive happy numbers; least happy of height h).
E35: Kimberling shuffle: In Kimberling’s “boxing and expelling” array process, is every positive integer eventually expelled?
E36: Klarner-Rado sequence density: For the thinnest set containing 1 and closed under x→2x, x→3x+2, x→6x+3, does the resulting sequence have positive density?
E37: Mousetrap (Cayley): Classify winning permutations and characterize reformed permutations; can reformation chains be arbitrarily long and can nontrivial cycles occur?
E38: Odd binary sequences: For which lengths n do there exist 0-1 sequences whose cyclic autocorrelation sums are all odd (and when they exist, how many are there)?
