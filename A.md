A1: Prime values of quadratic functions: Are there infinitely many primes of the form a^2 + 1?
A2: Factorial/primorial primes: Are there infinitely many primes of the form n!±1 or p#±1 (p# = ∏_{q≤p} q)?
A3: Mersenne primes, repunits, Fermat numbers, primes of shape k * 2^n + 1: Are there infinitely many primes of these special forms?
A4: Prime number race: For coprime a,b, are there infinitely many n where primes ≤ n favor residue a (mod b) over every other residue class?
A5: Arithmetic progressions of primes: How long can an arithmetic progression of primes be -- are there arbitrarily long arithmetic progressions of primes?
A6: Consecutive primes in arithmetic progression: Can there be arbitrarily long arithmetic progressions consisting of consecutive primes?
A7: Cunningham chains: Are there arbitrarily long chains of primes where each is 2p + 1 (or 2p - 1) of the previous?
A8: Gaps between primes / twin primes: How large and how small can prime gaps be -- are there infinitely many twin primes?
A9: Patterns of primes: Are there infinitely many sets of consecutive primes in any admissible pattern (prime k-tuples)?
A10: Gilbreath's conjecture: Do the iterated absolute differences of the primes always start with 1?
A11: Increasing and decreasing gaps: Are there infinitely many runs of three consecutive prime gaps that are strictly increasing or strictly decreasing?
A12: Pseudoprimes: Are there any square pseudoprimes (and how do pseudoprimes to various bases grow)?
A13: Carmichael numbers: What is the growth of C_k(x), the count with exactly k prime factors (e.g., is it essentially x^(1/k))?
A14: "Good" primes and the prime number graph: Does the set of n with P_n good have density 0 (and related inequalities)?
A15: Congruent products of consecutive numbers: For a prime p, can multiple blocks of consecutive integers have products congruent to 1 mod p (for arbitrarily many blocks)?
A16: Gaussian and Eisenstein-Jacobi primes: Can one walk to infinity using these primes with bounded step size?
A17: Formulas for primes: Is there a simple explicit formula or criterion for primes (e.g., for pi(x) or the nth prime)?
A18: Erdos-Selfridge classification of primes: Are there infinitely many primes in each class, and how does the least prime in class r grow?
A19: Prime shifts by powers of 2: Find n such that n-2^k is prime for every 2^k<n; also find the least odd not of the form ±p^a±2^b.
A20: Symmetric and asymmetric primes: Characterize pairs (p,q) with S(p,q)=S(q,p) and estimate how many symmetric primes there are.
